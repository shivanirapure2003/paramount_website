Wall & Ceiling Primer

Wall and ceiling primer is a preparatory coating applied before painting to ensure a smooth, adhesive-friendly surface. Acting as a sealant, it prevents uneven absorption, enhances paint adhesion, and increases the durability and visual consistency of the final finish. Ideal for both new surfaces and repainting jobs, primers are a must-have for professional-grade painting results.

USES

Wall and ceiling primers are essential for:

  • Preparing new drywall, plaster, or textured surfaces
  • Covering stains, marks, or strong existing colors before repainting
  • Sealing porous surfaces to reduce paint absorption
  • Ensuring strong paint adhesion on ceilings, interior walls, and even masonry
  • Creating a uniform base for light or drastic color transitions

METHOD OF USE

Surface Preparation

  • Clean the surface to remove dust, grease, mildew, or peeling paint
  • Patch holes and sand uneven areas for a smoother base
  • Ensure the surface is dry and structurally sound before application

Application

  • Apply with a brush, roller, or spray gun depending on surface and area size
  • One uniform coat is usually sufficient, but a second coat may be applied if needed
  • Let dry fully before applying the topcoat (typically 1–4 hours depending on type and humidity)

KEY FEATURES

  • Enhanced Adhesion: Creates a bond between the surface and paint, reducing flaking and peeling
  • Uniform Finish: Smooths out minor surface flaws and evens color absorption
  • Sealing Power: Blocks stains, odors, and porous spots for clean coverage
  • Improved Paint Efficiency: Reduces the number of topcoats needed, saving time and material
  • Stain & Odor Resistance: Especially with stain-blocking primers, ideal for ceilings or problem walls
  • Compatibility: Available in variants suitable for drywall, cement, textured finishes, and ceilings

PRECAUTIONS

  • Always use in well-ventilated areas and wear protective gear
  • Test primer on a small area to check for compatibility or reactions
  • Avoid application on damp, greasy, or unstable surfaces

STORAGE

Store in dry cool place in the temperature range from 5⁰C to 30⁰C in sealed condition. Do not allow to freeze. Keep away from direct sunlight. SHELF LIFE – 18 Months

HEALTH & SAFETY

Exercise caution and use protective clothing such as gloves and goggles. Inhalation of vapor or mist should be avoided. If inhaled symptoms include coughing, wheezing, laryngitis, and shortness of breath, headache, nausea, and vomiting. Immediately shift victim to fresh air & if need be start artificial respiration. Give oxygen if breathing is labored & call emergency medical help. In case of eye contact flush with water for 15 minutes and call for medical help. If swallowed will cause nausea, vomiting, and loss of consciousness. Do not induce vomiting rather call for medical help. If contact with skin flush with water/soap & water until all traces are removed. Seek medical attention if required.
